# Assessment of Enforcement Operations Against "Narco-Terrorism" in the Americas

## Definition and Scope

The concept of "narco-terrorism" is a contentious and ambiguous term, with varying definitions across governments and international organizations. The United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) defines narco-terrorism as "the use of violence and intimidation by organized crime groups to further their objectives" [Source 1]. However, other definitions, such as that provided by the Organization of American States (OAS), focus more on the economic and political aspects of organized crime groups' activities [Source 2].

## Legal Framework

Enforcement operations against alleged "narco-terrorism" in the Americas raise significant legal concerns. The use of force and military action can be justified under the principles of self-defense and the enforcement of international law [Source 3]. However, the sovereignty of nations is also a critical concern, as enforcement operations can be seen as a form of extrajudicial action [Source 4]. The UN Charter and the Rome Statute of the International Criminal Court (ICC) provide frameworks for international cooperation and the prosecution of international crimes, but their application to "narco-terrorism" is unclear [Source 5].

### Colombia: Operation Martillo

Operation Martillo, launched in 2012, was a joint military operation between Colombia, the United States, and other regional partners aimed at targeting narco-trafficking groups. The operation led to the seizure of large quantities of cocaine and the disruption of key trafficking routes [Source 6]. However, the operation also raised concerns about the impact on civilian populations and the potential for unintended consequences, such as the displacement of rural communities [Source 7].

### Mexico: The War on Drugs

Mexico's war on drugs, launched in 2006, has resulted in significant violence and human rights abuses, including the deaths of thousands of civilians and security personnel [Source 8]. The war on drugs has also led to the rise of powerful cartels, which have exploited the chaos to further their activities [Source 9]. The effectiveness of the war on drugs has been questioned, with some arguing that it has not led to a significant reduction in drug trafficking or organized crime [Source 10].
